Gear cycle

Post by : Den 20-12-2019

A "gear cycle" typically refers to a bicycle equipped with gears, allowing riders to adjust the resistance and optimize efficiency while pedaling. Gearing systems consist of multiple sprockets (gears) at the rear wheel and sometimes at the pedals, along with a derailleur mechanism to shift between them. This setup enables cyclists to tackle various terrains and inclines more comfortably by choosing the appropriate gear ratio. Whether for leisurely rides, commuting, or competitive cycling, gear cycles offer versatility and improved performance for riders of all skill levels.
